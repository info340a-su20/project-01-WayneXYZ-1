natural disaster observant

Proposed by Wayne Zhang

Problem / Data Domain

Overview

Although it did not get much attention over mainstream media, natural disasters occur very frequently around the world and affect many people. From personal experience, I would get information of some significant natural disasters from the New York Times, the only major news agency, but the information often comes in the form of notification stripes, and users are less likely to acquire broader information regarding the disaster (i.e. associated / similar events) as the reports were often concentrated on that specific disaster.

Natural disaster is an issue that stands above politics or society; it is something we humans should face together. Current resources lack up-to-date records, and news reporting services often would not provide a unified place specifically for natural disasters.

IT | Existing Similar Products

Acquiring a comprehensive set of natural disaster data would require researching on major agencies or institutes around the world whose results could cover as much of the globe as possible. An existing website thoroughly covers the result and data visualizations of disasters prior to 2019; however, its interactability is very limited: the contents on the website are designed to present long texts that explain stuff, and interactive visualizations are put at a very low position. This illustrates the difficulties of developing a product that could self-update and is oriented toward interaction and association of natural disaster. With the interaction state-of-mind, the process could involve model making as the first step. I believe a unified source of such information would also make it easiers for NGOs to work on their agendas of helping with the disasters when governments fail to respond.

App Description

App Content

With an empathetic mind, I have thought of a platform that fills such a gap throughout the past year. The product would contain relatively more comprehensive-in-width information regarding natural disasters. I envisioned the web app to:

- contain up-to-date information of various types of natural disasters;
- give the user related information, such as impact on societal and economic levels;
- providing related disasters and comparison in the past;

The website would be a combination of interactive dashboard and documentation. As referenced above, an existing website presents well-explained background knowledge to natural disasters. Attempting to extend what it did well, I want to promote the weights of interactive elements, add more tables and charts that present relative information in a more tailored way.

User Story

I imagine the users of this web app to care about the nature and its interaciton with human to a certain extent, afterall, it's a disaster platform. The user might be someone working for an environmental assistant or humanitarian NGO, a student studying related fields, or even a researcher looking for some primary data. Perhaps the user would read the description of a certain natural disaster or compare two or more similar events, cross-evaluate their impact on the people. They might use the economic impact or governmental response comparison as a source for their research in sociology, environmental study, or political science.

Information | Interaction

Given the self-update nature I envisioned for the web app, it could draw data from geographic institutes or disaster control centers who disclose and update their information. The app's goal would be to cover as much area of the world as possible, not leaving anyone behind. This means the source-selection will need to be re-examined once in a while to ensure that the institute is still providing up-to-date information. The essential guarantee of the web app will be a sustainable and easy-to-navigate interaction between the information of each disaster and the data visualizations and components that unifies them.

In short, when designing the web app, it would be beared in mind that the user will need as little effort as possible to navigate through the interactions and different types of disaster data analysis would be related in a straight-forward way: the user will need a low amount of input to get what they want (and what they can get).
